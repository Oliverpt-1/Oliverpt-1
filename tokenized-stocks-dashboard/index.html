Methodology

Data

Every Solana DEX trade involving a Backed xStock token (mint prefixed Xs…, ticker suffixed …x) from Dune's dex_solana.trades, over the trailing 365 days. Six SQL queries run on Dune power every chart on this page.

Classifying a wallet as a bot

A wallet is labelled a bot if it meets any of:

  • Arbitrage / MM — bought and sold the same stock inside one Solana slot (~400 ms). Humans can't round-trip an asset within a single block.
  • High-frequency — averages ≥ 50 trades per active day.
  • High-volume — ≥ 1,000 trades in the window.

Everything else is retail. The median retail wallet makes just a couple of trades over the entire window.

Caveats

Heuristics, not ground truth: a sophisticated human is indistinguishable from a slow bot, and one person can run many wallets. Centralized-exchange (e.g. Kraken) flow that settles off-chain isn't captured. Volume is DEX swap notional and can include intermediate routing hops.
